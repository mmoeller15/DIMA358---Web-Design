Driver Monitoring System

The goal of the proposed system was to monitor and identify inattentiveness and drowsiness in drivers by way of electromyography (EMG) to monitor eye blink rate, electroencephalography (EEG) to monitor brain activity, and electrocardiography (ECG) signals to monitor heart rate. Through this research it was determined that single-channel EEG from the channel F7 electrode, placed near the temple above the frontal lobe, was a suitable and convenient location for determining drowsiness and alertness in drivers, producing an average accuracy of 67.378%. From the observed data it was possible to locate and identify decernable patterns indicative of drowsy and alert states in the F7 channel. These results furthered the goal of creating an accessible, non-invasive, compact system able to automatically detect distracted and drowsy driving to act as a warning system, alerting users of inattentive and dangerous behavior.

Wearable Posture Monitoring System

The goal of the proposed system was to automatically track a user's postural positions throughout the day through the use of a non-invasive, wearable system and automatically provide feedback to change their posture. This was done by placing magnets in the form of a rectangular grid on a shirt as well as an MMR sensor on the chest of the body. The onboard magnetic sensor records the data values from the grid of magnetics, which is then, along with data recorded from the onboard accelerometer, analyzed to determine the position of the user. A trained algorithm recognizes and automatically detects the spinal position of the user from the recorded data points and provides direction to alter posture. These recommendations act as a warning system and allow the user to self-monitor to prevent back and neck pain and reduce the chance of long-lasting damage that can result from poor posture.
